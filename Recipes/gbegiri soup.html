Gbegiri soup

Description

Gbegiri soup is a type of Nigerian soup popularly eaten by the Yoruba tribe of Nigeria. It is made from peeled cooked bean.

Ingredients

  • black-eyed peas or brown beans
  • peeled Cayenne pepper or fresh pepper
  • palm oil
  • Ground crayfish
  • Seasoning (stock cubes)
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Boil assorted meat till tender with seasoning and salt. Separate stock from meat and set aside
  2. Blend cooked beans in a blender for a smooth puree and set aside
  3. Place a cooking pan on medium heat, add palm oil and heat for about 3 minutes
  4. Stir puréed beans in palm oil (beans purée should be somewhere in the middle of loose and thick) Add water if needed to achieve the desired thickness
  5. Add assorted beef and cayenne pepper, stir till well combined. Reduce the heat and leave to cook for about 10 minutes. Check-in between to avoid soup from burning
  6. Add crayfish, seasoning and salt. At this point don’t want the soup to be thick, add water if need be but don’t overdo it.
  7. Continue to cook until all ingredients are well combined. Check for seasoning and salt.
